# Meeting Minutes

## Meeting Overview
- Date: October 9, 2017
- Location: Denver City Council Chambers
- Attendees: Councilman Lopez, Councilman Clark, Councilwoman Ortega, Councilwoman Keneach, Councilman Martega, Madam Secretary Rokall, and others (exact names not specified).

## Summary
The Denver City Council meeting focused on the observance of Indigenous Peoples Day, including the reading and adoption of Proclamation 1127. Council members discussed the significance of the proclamation, the contributions of Indigenous peoples, and the importance of cultural preservation and inclusivity.

## Key Discussion Points
- The symbolism behind the logo for Indigenous Peoples Day and its connection to water and the confluence of rivers.
- Announcement of the first Halloween parade in District 7 on October 21.
- Reading of Proclamation 1127, which recognizes Indigenous Peoples Day in Denver.
- The historical context of Indigenous peoples in Denver and their contributions to the community.
- The importance of cultural preservation and the role of Indigenous peoples in environmental protection.
- Expressions of solidarity with Indigenous communities and the significance of inclusivity.

## Decisions
- Proclamation 1127 was adopted by the Council, officially designating October 9, 2017, as Indigenous Peoples Day in Denver.

No clear action items identified.

## Takeaways
- The meeting highlighted the importance of recognizing and honoring Indigenous peoples and their contributions to Denver's history and culture.
- Council members expressed a commitment to inclusivity and solidarity with Indigenous communities.
- The adoption of Proclamation 1127 marks a significant step in acknowledging the cultural heritage of Indigenous peoples in Denver.
