## Summary
The Denver City Council meeting focused on the observance of Indigenous Peoples Day, including the reading and adoption of Proclamation 1127. Council members discussed the significance of the day and the contributions of Indigenous peoples to the city. Councilman Lopez emphasized the importance of pride in one's culture and the ongoing challenges faced by Indigenous communities.

## Key Discussion Points
- Introduction of the meeting and Pledge of Allegiance.
- Approval of the minutes from the previous meeting.
- Announcement of the first Halloween parade in District 7 on October 21st.
- Reading of Proclamation 1127 in observance of Indigenous Peoples Day.
- Discussion on the contributions of Indigenous peoples to Denver's history and culture.
- Emphasis on the need for continued support and recognition of Indigenous communities.

## Decisions
- Proclamation 1127, recognizing October 9, 2017, as Indigenous Peoples Day in Denver, was adopted.

## Action Items
No clear action items identified.

## Takeaways
- The council reaffirmed its commitment to recognizing and celebrating Indigenous Peoples Day.
- Council members highlighted the importance of cultural pride and the contributions of Indigenous peoples to the community.
- There is a need for ongoing efforts to address the challenges faced by Indigenous communities in Denver.
