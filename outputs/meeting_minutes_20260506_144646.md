## Summary
The Denver City Council meeting focused on the observance of Indigenous Peoples Day, including the reading and adoption of Proclamation 1127. Council members discussed the significance of the day and the contributions of Indigenous peoples to the community. Councilman Lopez emphasized the importance of pride in one's culture and the ongoing challenges faced by Indigenous communities.

## Key Discussion Points
- Introduction of the meeting and Pledge of Allegiance.
- Approval of the minutes from the previous meeting.
- Announcement of the first Halloween parade in District 7 on October 21st.
- Reading of Proclamation 1127 in observance of Indigenous Peoples Day.
- Discussion on the contributions of Indigenous peoples to Denver and the importance of recognizing their history and culture.
- Emphasis on the need for continued support and action for Indigenous communities.

## Decisions
- Proclamation 1127, in observance of Indigenous Peoples Day, was adopted.

## Takeaways
- The Denver City Council officially recognizes October 9th as Indigenous Peoples Day.

- The meeting highlighted the contributions of Indigenous peoples to the history and development of Denver.
